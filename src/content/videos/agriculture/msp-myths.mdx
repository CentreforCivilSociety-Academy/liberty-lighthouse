This session interrogates the economics of India's Minimum Support Price (MSP) regime — its origins in the food security imperatives of the 1960s, its current reach and limitations, and the distributional consequences of procurement-based price support. The discussion addresses common misconceptions about MSP coverage, examines which crops and regions actually benefit from government procurement, and evaluates alternative mechanisms for supporting farmer incomes, including direct benefit transfers and crop insurance reform.
