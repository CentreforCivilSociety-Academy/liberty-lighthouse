This lecture examines the three agricultural reform laws enacted in 2020 — the Farmers' Produce Trade and Commerce Act, the Farmers' Agreement on Price Assurance Act, and the Essential Commodities (Amendment) Act. The discussion covers the economic rationale for dismantling APMC monopolies, the intended effects on farmer income and market access, the political economy of agricultural reform in India, and the circumstances leading to the laws' repeal in November 2021. The session draws on comparative evidence from states that had already undertaken partial market reforms, as well as international models of agricultural liberalisation.
