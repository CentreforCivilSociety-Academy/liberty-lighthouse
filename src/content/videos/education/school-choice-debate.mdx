Prof. James Tooley draws on his extensive fieldwork across India, sub-Saharan Africa, and China to make the case for school choice as a driver of educational quality. The presentation opens with findings from his research in the old city of Hyderabad, where the majority of children attended unrecognized private schools (0:00 - 7:00), proceeds to comparative evidence on learning outcomes between government and low-cost private schools (7:00 - 14:30), examines the regulatory barriers that constrain the growth of affordable private education (14:30 - 19:00), and concludes with a discussion of policy mechanisms — including vouchers, direct benefit transfers, and deregulation — that could expand meaningful choice for Indian families (19:00 - 24:15).
