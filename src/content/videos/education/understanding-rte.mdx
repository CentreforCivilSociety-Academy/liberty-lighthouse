In this talk, Dr. Parth Shah examines the Right to Education Act from its constitutional origins through its on-the-ground consequences. The discussion covers the historical context of Article 21A and the legislative process behind the 2009 Act (0:00 - 4:30), a detailed walkthrough of key provisions including input norms, the no-detention policy, and Section 12(1)(c) reservations (4:30 - 10:15), empirical evidence on school closures and the impact on budget private schools serving low-income communities (10:15 - 14:50), and a concluding analysis of alternative regulatory frameworks that prioritize learning outcomes over infrastructure mandates (14:50 - 18:42).
